Symptoms of Stress

Photo: Practo

This is a list of the symptoms of stress.

Make sure to check and see if you have any of these symptoms.
If you have a few of these symptoms, you're probably stressed out. If that's the case, don't worry as you'll later see how to deal with stress.

  • Dry mouth and difficulty swallowing
  • Nervousness or shaking
  • Constant ringing in the ear
  • Cold and sweaty/clammy hands and feet
  • Insomnia
  • Chest pain
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Muscle aches and pains
  • Tense muscles
  • Headaches
  • Nausea
  • Weak immune system resulting in frequent cold and infections
  • Diarrhoea or constipation

Long-term Effects of Stress

Photo: Anxiety and Depression Association of America

Mental Disorders

If taken care of early, stress is not a thing to worry about. However, when your stress is neglected, it can become chronic. Chronic stress can potentially develop into more severe mental disorders, such as but not limited to:

  • Depression
  • Anxiety
  • Psychosis (A severe disorder where one loses contact with reality)

Physical Health Issues

Chronic stress can also affect the physical body significantly, like:

  • Cardiovascular Diseases
  • Increased risk of Heart Attack
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)
  • High blood sugar and an Increased risk of developing Diabetes

Scary! Well don't be afraid. There is a solution for every problem, and stress is no different. In the next page, you'll learn how to deal with stress and channel this in a positive manner.
